                            A. PROBLEM FORMULATION


 --------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Background of the Problem
   Telecommunication shelters are vital infrastructure facilities that function as locations for operating and protecting network equipment, including GPON (Gigabit Passive Optical Network) devices, to ensure reliable and continuous telecommunication services. For a company such as Telkom, the condition of the shelter has a significant impact on the quality of services delivered to customers. Challenges faced in the shelter can impact network reliability and customer experience.
   From the customer's perspective, problems inside the shelter are not physically visible, however, their impacts are clearly experienced. Customers may encounter unstable internet connections, reduced service quality, increased latency, or sudden service interruptions. These conditions disrupt customer activities and reduce confidence in the reliability of the services provided.
   One of the primary engineering problems in telecommunication shelters is suboptimal temperature management. Network equipment, particularly GPON devices such as OLTs and optical modules, is highly sensitive to environmental temperature. Uncontrolled temperature increases inside the shelter can lead to performance degradation, transmission instability, and automatic shutdowns as protective mechanisms. From the customer's point of view, this results in unexpected service disruptions without prior indication.
   In addition to temperature, excessive vibration within the shelter is another engineering issue that can affect the reliability of GPON equipment. Vibration may be caused by heavy vehicle traffic around the shelter, nearby mechanical activities, or structural conditions of the shelter itself. Continuous vibration can loosen fiber optic connectors, disturb the stability of optical modules, and degrade GPON transmission quality.
   From the customer perspective, the impact of vibration is often experienced as intermittent service disturbances, such as frequent connection drops, sudden signal quality degradation, increased packet loss, or loss of signal (LOS) events. These issues are difficult to identify because they do not always coincide with visible hardware failures.
   The lack of real-time monitoring systems for shelter conditions, particularly temperature and vibration, makes early detection of these problems challenging. As a result, maintenance actions tend to be reactive, occurring only after customers experience service degradation. Therefore, an engineering solution is required to continuously and real-time monitor temperature and vibration within Telkom shelters to maintain GPON equipment stability, minimize service disruptions, and improve overall customer experience.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 Problem from a Customer Perspective
   From Telkom's perspective as the operator and owner of telecommunication shelters, maintaining the reliability and integrity of shelter infrastructure is a critical operational responsibility. Telkom manages a large number of shelters that are geographically distributed across wide areas, many of which are located in remote or difficult-to-access locations. Due to these conditions, continuous 24/7 on-site supervision by technicians is not feasible.
   The absence of continuous physical monitoring increases the risk that abnormal environmental conditions inside the shelter, such as elevated temperature and excessive vibration, may go undetected for extended periods. From Telkom's operational standpoint, this creates potential vulnerabilities that can lead to equipment degradation, reduced lifespan of GPON devices, and increased likelihood of network-related incidents if not addressed promptly.
   Furthermore, the wide distance between shelter locations limits the efficiency of manual inspection and reactive maintenance. When environmental issues are detected late, Telkom may face increased maintenance costs, unplanned corrective actions, and higher operational workload for technical teams. These challenges highlight the difficulty of ensuring consistent shelter conditions using conventional monitoring approaches.
   As a result, Telkom requires a preventive and centralized approach to monitor shelter conditions in order to reduce the probability of network disturbances and physical damage to GPON equipment. The lack of continuous supervision at geographically dispersed shelter sites emphasizes the need for a remote monitoring solution that supports proactive maintenance and operational efficiency.

 --------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Problem Statement
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
   
 Telkom operates telecommunication shelters that are geographically distributed across wide areas, making continuous 24/7 on-site supervision by technicians impractical.
 Due to this limitation, environmental conditions inside the shelter, particularly temperature and vibration, cannot be monitored in real time using conventional methods.
 Uncontrolled temperature and excessive vibration have the potential to accelerate the degradation of GPON equipment, reduce equipment lifespan, and increase the risk of equipment damage within the shelter.
 Detection of abnormal shelter conditions is often delayed, resulting in reactive maintenance actions rather than preventive measures.
 The absence of an integrated monitoring system limits Telkom's ability to perform proactive infrastructure management and efficient technical decision-making.


 --------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Objective
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
   
 To design and implement a continuous and real-time monitoring system for temperature and vibration in telecommunication shelters.
 To enable early detection of abnormal environmental conditions that may affect GPON equipment.
 To reduce the likelihood of network disturbances and physical damage to shelter equipment caused by temperature and vibration factors.
 To support preventive maintenance strategies and improve operational efficiency for technical teams.
 To provide accurate and reliable shelter condition data to support engineering analysis and maintenance planning.





                              B. PROBLEM ANALYSIS

   1. Aspect 1: Operational (Monitoring and Maintenance)
   Telecommunication shelters are distributed across wide geographical areas, making continuous 24/7 on-site monitoring by technicians impractical. This operational limitation constrains Telkom's ability to directly observe shelter conditions such as temperature and vibration in real time. As a result, abnormal conditions may not be detected immediately, increasing the risk of delayed maintenance actions. Any proposed solution must address the limitation of manual supervision and support remote, continuous monitoring.
   2. Aspect 2: Technical (Equipment Reliability)
   GPON equipment installed inside the shelter is sensitive to environmental factors, particularly excessive temperature and vibration. These conditions can accelerate equipment degradation and reduce operational lifespan. The technical constraint lies in detecting environmental changes that may affect equipment reliability without interfering with existing network operations. Therefore, the solution must be technically compatible with current GPON infrastructure and capable of providing accurate and reliable measurements.
   3. Aspect 3: Infrastructure and Integration
   Shelter monitoring solutions must be integrated into existing shelter infrastructure without requiring major physical modifications. Space limitations, power availability, and existing layout constraints restrict the type of monitoring devices that can be installed. This aspect limits the design flexibility of the system and requires a compact, non-intrusive, and easily deployable solution.
   4. Aspect 4: Data and Response Time
   Timely data availability is critical for preventive maintenance. Delays in data transmission or lack of real-time alerts reduce the effectiveness of monitoring and increase the likelihood of equipment damage. This constraint highlights the need for reliable data communication and fast response mechanisms to support early decision-making by Telkom's technical teams.
   5. Aspect 5: Scalability and Efficiency
   Telkom manages a large number of shelters with limited operational resources. The monitoring solution must be scalable to support multiple shelter locations while maintaining efficiency in data management and system maintenance. This aspect constrains the solution to be centralized, manageable, and cost-efficient when deployed at scale.

                     C. SOLUTION SELECTION & SCENARIOS

Alternative Solution 1 - Smart CCTV System with AI-Based Human Detection
The solution is given to the problem.
In this alternative, CCTV cameras are enhanced with artificial intelligence to detect human presence and identify whether a person is authorized or unauthorized. The system automatically analyzes video footage and sends alerts when suspicious activity is detected. This solution improves security compared to traditional CCTV but focuses mainly on visual monitoring and does not address environmental and vibration-based risks that directly affect GPON equipment reliability.

Alternative Solution 2 - Periodic Data Logging with Offline Analysis 
This solution uses sensors to record vibration and environmental data continuously but stores the data locally in a data logger. Technicians retrieve the data periodically for offline analysis to identify trends and equipment issues. While this approach is low-cost and simple, it does not support real-time monitoring or immediate alerts, making it unsuitable for preventing sudden failures or security incidents.

Alternative Solution 3 - IoT-Based Environmental and Vibration Monitoring with AI
This solution integrates vibration sensors and environmental sensors with an IoT communication system to continuously monitor GPON shelter conditions. The collected data is transmitted in real time to a central server where an AI model analyzes vibration patterns and environmental trends. The AI classifies the cause of vibration, such as human activity, vehicle movement, or seismic events, and predicts the potential risk of G-PON equipment damage. In addition, CCTV cameras are used as visual sensors to support the AI analysis by providing visual confirmation of human presence and activities inside the shelter. When high-risk conditions are detected, the system automatically sends alerts to maintenance teams through a messaging platform and displays the results on a web-based dashboard.
This approach focuses on proactive maintenance and risk prediction rather than reactive monitoring.



Solution Selection
Based on the three alternatives, Alternative Solution 3  -  IoT-Based Environmental and Vibration Monitoring with AI is selected.
Alternative Solution 1 is limited by its reliance on manual monitoring and delayed response. Alternative Solution 2 improves security through intelligent CCTV but does not address the primary technical risks faced by GPON equipment, such as vibration and environmental conditions. Alternative Solution 3 provides a more comprehensive and scalable solution by directly monitoring the physical factors that affect GPON reliability while also utilizing camera sensors as visual support to validate detected activities and applying AI to analyze patterns and predict potential damage. Therefore, this solution is chosen as the most appropriate approach for improving GPON shelter monitoring and maintenance.
